01 — The Problem

A TMS machine on the floor is not a TMS program.

i.

Insurance authorization is the silent killer

Most practices lose 3–6 months of revenue on denied or delayed auths because their intake, documentation, and appeal workflows weren't built for TMS-specific payer rules.

ii.

Untrained technicians hurt outcomes

Motor threshold determination, coil placement, and session documentation require real clinical competence. Mistakes show up as side effects, non-response, and protocol drift.

iii.

Workflows built for meds don't work for TMS

A 30-session treatment course over 6–8 weeks demands scheduling, tracking, and outcome measurement systems most psychiatry practices simply don't have yet.
